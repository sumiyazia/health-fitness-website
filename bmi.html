BMI FAQ

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It's a measure of body fat based on height and weight that applies to both men and women. It's calculated by dividing weight (kg) by height squared (m²).
BMI has limitations for athletes and muscular individuals since it doesn't differentiate between muscle and fat. Athletes may have a higher BMI despite being healthy due to muscle mass being heavier than fat.
Improve your BMI through a combination of healthy eating, regular exercise, and lifestyle changes. Consult with nutritionists and fitness professionals for personalized guidance tailored to your goals.
BMI is a useful screening tool but shouldn't be your only health metric. Factors like fitness level, muscle mass, and overall lifestyle also matter. Consult healthcare professionals for comprehensive health assessment.
